# Biofilms

This document discusses the structure and function of biofilms.
In biofilms, microbial cells are embedded in a self-produced extracellular matrix that protects them from environmental hazards, and allows them to communicate with each other.

## Properties of Biofilms

- **Formation**: Biofilms form on various surfaces through a **multistep process** involving:
  1. Initial attachment of microorganisms to a surface.
  2. Production of extracellular polymeric substances (EPS).
  3. Growth and maturation into complex structures.

- **Mathematical Representation**: The dynamics of biofilm growth can be modeled using differential equations, such as:
  $$\frac{dX}{dt} = \mu \cdot (S - K) \cdot X$$
  Where:
  - $X$: biomass concentration (g/m^3)
  - $S$: substrate concentration (g/m^3)
  - $K$: half-saturation constant (g/m^3)
  - $\mu$: maximum specific growth rate (1/day)

## Conclusion
Biofilms are crucial to natural and engineered ecosystems due to their ability to stabilize and enhance biological processes. Further understanding of their dynamics can improve bioengineering applications.
